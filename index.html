    Biography

I am currently a Specially Appointed Assistant Professor in the Hatanaka Lab, Department of Systems and Control Engineering at Tokyo Institute of Technology, Japan. I received my B.S. degree in Physics from Zhejiang University, China, in 2016 and my Ph.D. degree in Electrical and Electronic Engineering under the supervision of Prof. Graziano Chesi, from the University of Hong Kong in 2020. I was a visiting student in Prof. Yiguang Hong's lab at the Academy of Mathematics and Systems Science, Chinese Academy of Sciences (CAS), China. From September 2019 to December 2019, I was a visiting researcher in School of Engineering, Osaka University, Osaka, Japan, working with Prof. Takeshi Hatanaka. From October 2020 to January 2021, I was a Post-doctoral Fellow at Hong Kong University of Science and Technology, working with Prof. Li Qiu. From February 2021 to July 2022, I served as a research associate with the Control Group at the University of Cambridge, working with Prof. Ioannis Lestas. My research interests include optimization, robust control, and power systems.
